## 2. Ampere's Law — Solution

We use the magnetic field due to a long straight wire:

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}
$$

---

## 1. Given

- Distance between wires: $0.10\,\text{m}$
- Midpoint distance: $r = 0.05\,\text{m}$
- Current: $I = 5\,\text{A}$
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}$

---

## 2. Field from One Wire

$$
B_1 = \frac{4\pi \times 10^{-7} \cdot 5}{2\pi \cdot 0.05}
$$

Simplify:

$$
B_1 = \frac{20\pi \times 10^{-7}}{0.1\pi}
= 2 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{T}
$$

---

## 3. Total Field at Midpoint

Since currents are in **opposite directions**, their magnetic fields at the midpoint point in the **same direction**, so they add:

$$
B_{\text{total}} = 2B_1 = 4 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{T}
$$

---

## 4. Direction

Using the right-hand rule:
- Fields from both wires point **into the page** at the midpoint

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{B = 4 \times 10^{-5}\,\text{T}}
$$

**Direction:** Into the page
