## 3. Biot–Savart Law — Solution

We use the Biot–Savart Law for a small current element:

$$
dB = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I\,dl\,\sin\theta}{r^2}
$$

---

## 1. Given

- $I = 3\,\text{A}$
- $dl = 0.1\,\text{m}$
- $r = 0.2\,\text{m}$
- $\theta = 90^\circ \Rightarrow \sin\theta = 1$
- $\frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} = 10^{-7}$

---

## 2. Substitute Values

$$
B = 10^{-7} \cdot \frac{(3)(0.1)(1)}{(0.2)^2}
$$

---

## 3. Calculate

$$
B = 10^{-7} \cdot \frac{0.3}{0.04}
= 10^{-7} \cdot 7.5
$$

$$
B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7}\,\text{T}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7}\,\text{T}}
$$

**Direction:** Given by the right-hand rule (perpendicular to both the current element and the line to point $P$).
