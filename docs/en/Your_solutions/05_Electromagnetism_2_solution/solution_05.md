## 5. Energy Stored in a Capacitor — Solution

Given:
- $S = 0.02\,\text{m}^2$
- $d = 5\,\text{mm} = 5 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{m}$
- $V = 500\,\text{V}$
- $\varepsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^{-12}$

---

## 1. Capacitance

For a parallel-plate capacitor:

$$
C = \varepsilon_0 \frac{S}{d}
$$

$$
C = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \cdot \frac{0.02}{5 \times 10^{-3}}
$$

$$
C = 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \cdot 4
= 3.54 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{F}
$$

---

## 2. Energy Stored

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2
$$

$$
U = \frac{1}{2} (3.54 \times 10^{-11})(500)^2
$$

$$
U = 0.5 \cdot 3.54 \times 10^{-11} \cdot 2.5 \times 10^5
$$

$$
U \approx 4.43 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{J}
$$

---

## 3. Electric Field

$$
E = \frac{V}{d}
$$

$$
E = \frac{500}{5 \times 10^{-3}} = 1.0 \times 10^5\,\text{V/m}
$$

---

## 4. Force Between Plates

Using energy method:

$$
F = \frac{1}{2} \varepsilon_0 E^2 S
$$

$$
F = \frac{1}{2} (8.85 \times 10^{-12})(1.0 \times 10^5)^2 (0.02)
$$

$$
F = 0.5 \cdot 8.85 \times 10^{-12} \cdot 10^{10} \cdot 0.02
$$

$$
F \approx 8.85 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{N}
$$

---

## Final Answers

- Capacitance: $\boxed{3.54 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{F}}$
- Energy: $\boxed{4.43 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{J}}$
- Electric field: $\boxed{1.0 \times 10^5\,\text{V/m}}$
- Force: $\boxed{8.85 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{N}}$
