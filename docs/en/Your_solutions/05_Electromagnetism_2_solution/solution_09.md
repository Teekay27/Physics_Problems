## 9. Refraction (Snell's Law) — Solution

Use Snell’s Law:

$$
n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2
$$

---

## 1. Given

- $n_1 = 1.00$ (air)  
- $n_2 = 1.50$ (glass)  
- $\theta_1 = 30^\circ$

---

## 2. Solve for $\theta_2$

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{n_1}{n_2} \sin\theta_1
= \frac{1.00}{1.50} \sin 30^\circ
$$

$$
\sin\theta_2 = \frac{1}{1.5} \cdot 0.5
= \frac{1}{3}
$$

---

## 3. Calculate Angle

$$
\theta_2 = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)
\approx 19.5^\circ
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{\theta_2 \approx 19.5^\circ}
$$

**Note:** The ray bends **toward the normal** when entering a denser medium.
