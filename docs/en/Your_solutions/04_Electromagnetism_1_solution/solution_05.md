## 5. Field Levitation — Solution

To levitate a proton, the electric force must balance the gravitational force:

$$
F_e = F_g
$$

---

### 1. Forces

Electric force:
$$
F_e = qE
$$

Gravitational force:
$$
F_g = mg
$$

Set them equal:
$$
qE = mg
$$

---

### 2. Solve for Electric Field

$$
E = \frac{mg}{q}
$$

Substitute values:
- $m = 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg}$
- $g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2$
- $q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}$

$$
E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

---

### 3. Calculate

$$
E \approx \frac{1.64 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}
$$

$$
E \approx 1.0 \times 10^{-7}\,\text{N/C}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{E \approx 1.0 \times 10^{-7}\,\text{N/C}}
$$

**Direction:** The electric field must point **upward** to counteract gravity.
