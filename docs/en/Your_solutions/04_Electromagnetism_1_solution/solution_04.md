## 4. Force Comparison — Solution

We compare the electric and gravitational forces between an electron and a proton at  
$r = 5.3 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{m}$.

---

### 1. Electric Force ($F_e$)

Using Coulomb’s Law:

$$
F_e = k \frac{e^2}{r^2}
$$

Where:
- $k = 8.99 \times 10^9 \,\text{N m}^2/\text{C}^2$
- $e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}$

$$
F_e = 8.99 \times 10^9 \cdot \frac{(1.60 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}
$$

$$
F_e \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8}\,\text{N}
$$

---

### 2. Gravitational Force ($F_g$)

Using Newton’s Law of Gravitation:

$$
F_g = G \frac{m_e m_p}{r^2}
$$

Where:
- $G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{N m}^2/\text{kg}^2$
- $m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\,\text{kg}$
- $m_p = 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg}$

$$
F_g = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \cdot \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}
$$

$$
F_g \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47}\,\text{N}
$$

---

### 3. Ratio

$$
\frac{F_e}{F_g} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}
$$

---

## Final Answer

- $F_e \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8}\,\text{N}$
- $F_g \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47}\,\text{N}$
- $\frac{F_e}{F_g} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}$
