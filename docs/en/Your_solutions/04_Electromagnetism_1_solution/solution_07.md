## 7. Cyclotron Motion — Solution

An electron is accelerated through a potential difference $V = 5000\,\text{V}$ and then enters a magnetic field $B = 0.1\,\text{T}$.

---

## 1. Find the Electron Speed

The kinetic energy gained equals electric potential energy:

$$
\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = eV
$$

Solve for $v$:

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m}}
$$

Substitute:
- $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\,\text{C}$
- $m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}\,\text{kg}$

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{2(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(5000)}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}}
$$

$$
v \approx 4.2 \times 10^7\,\text{m/s}
$$

---

## 2. Radius of Circular Motion

Magnetic force provides centripetal force:

$$
qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}
$$

Solve for $r$:

$$
r = \frac{mv}{qB}
$$

---

## 3. Substitute Values

$$
r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(4.2 \times 10^7)}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19})(0.1)}
$$

$$
r \approx 2.4 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{m}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{r \approx 2.4 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{m} = 2.4\,\text{mm}}
$$

The electron follows a circular path of radius about **2.4 mm**.
