## 10. Lorentz Force Acting on a Wire — Solution

The magnetic force on a current-carrying wire is:

$$
F = BIL \sin\theta
$$

Where:
- $B = 0.5\,\text{T}$
- $I = 10\,\text{A}$
- $L = 2.0\,\text{m}$

---

## 1. Compute Constant Part

$$
BIL = (0.5)(10)(2.0) = 10
$$

So:
$$
F = 10 \sin\theta
$$

---

## 2. Cases

### (a) $\theta = 90^\circ$

$$
\sin 90^\circ = 1
$$

$$
F = 10 \times 1 = 10\,\text{N}
$$

---

### (b) $\theta = 45^\circ$

$$
\sin 45^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707
$$

$$
F = 10 \times 0.707 \approx 7.07\,\text{N}
$$

---

### (c) $\theta = 0^\circ$

$$
\sin 0^\circ = 0
$$

$$
F = 0\,\text{N}
$$

---

## Final Answers

- (a) $\boxed{10\,\text{N}}$
- (b) $\boxed{7.07\,\text{N}}$
- (c) $\boxed{0\,\text{N}}$
