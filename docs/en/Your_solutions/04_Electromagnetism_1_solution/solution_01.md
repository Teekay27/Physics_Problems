# Coulomb’s Law – Force on Centrally Placed Charge


## Formula

$$
F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}
$$

Where:
- $k$ is Coulomb’s constant $(9.0 \times 10^9 \, \text{N m}^2/\text{C}^2)$
- $q_1$ and $q_2$ represent the magnitudes of the interacting charges
- $r$ denotes the separation between the two charges

---

## Step 1: Geometry of the Problem

- Square side length: $a = 1.0 \, \text{m}$

Distance from center to each corner:

$$
r = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \, \text{m}
$$

---

## Step 2: Force from One Charge

Each corner charge: $+1.0 \, \text{C}$  
Center charge: $-2.0 \, \text{C}$

Magnitude of force from one corner:

$$
F = k \frac{(1.0)(2.0)}{(1/\sqrt{2})^2}
$$

$$
F = 9.0 \times 10^9 \cdot \frac{2}{1/2}
= 9.0 \times 10^9 \cdot 4
= 3.6 \times 10^{10} \, \text{N}
$$

---

## Step 3: Direction of Forces

- Each force acts along the line from the center to a corner.
- Since the center charge is negative, forces are **attractive** (toward each corner).

---

## Step 4: Symmetry Argument

- The four forces are equal in magnitude.
- They are symmetrically distributed (at $90^\circ$ intervals).
- Their vector components cancel out.

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{F_{\text{net}} = 0 \, \text{N}}
$$

**Direction:** None (forces cancel completely due to symmetry)
