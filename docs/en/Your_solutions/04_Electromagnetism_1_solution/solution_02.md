# Electric Potential at the Center of a Square

We use the principle of superposition together with the formula for electric potential from a point charge.

## Formula

$$
V = k \frac{q}{r}
$$

For multiple charges:

$$
V_{\text{net}} = k \sum \frac{q_i}{r_i}
$$

---

## Step 1: Geometry

- Side length of square: $a = 1.0 \, \text{m}$
- Distance from center to each corner:

$$
r = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \, \text{m}
$$

All চার charges are the same distance from the center.

---

## Step 2: Charges

Charges placed at corners (in order):
- $+1 \, \text{C}$
- $-2 \, \text{C}$
- $+3 \, \text{C}$
- $-4 \, \text{C}$

---

## Step 3: Compute Total Potential

Since all distances are equal:

$$
V_{\text{net}} = k \frac{1}{r} (q_1 + q_2 + q_3 + q_4)
$$

Sum of charges:

$$
1 - 2 + 3 - 4 = -2 \, \text{C}
$$

---

## Step 4: Substitute Values

$$
V_{\text{net}} = 9.0 \times 10^9 \cdot \frac{-2}{1/\sqrt{2}}
$$

$$
V_{\text{net}} = 9.0 \times 10^9 \cdot (-2\sqrt{2})
$$

$$
V_{\text{net}} = -18\sqrt{2} \times 10^9
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
V_{\text{net}} \approx -2.55 \times 10^{10} \, \text{V}
$$
