## 3. Electrostatic Equilibrium — Solution

Let the distance between $q_1 = +4\text{C}$ and $q_2 = +9\text{C}$ be $2\,\text{m}$.  
Assume the equilibrium point is at a distance $x$ from $q_1$.  
Then the distance from $q_2$ is $(2 - x)$.

At equilibrium, the forces on $q_3$ cancel out:

$$
F_1 = F_2
$$

Using Coulomb’s Law:

$$
k \frac{q_1 q_3}{x^2} = k \frac{q_2 q_3}{(2 - x)^2}
$$

Cancel $k$ and $q_3$:

$$
\frac{q_1}{x^2} = \frac{q_2}{(2 - x)^2}
$$

Substitute values:

$$
\frac{4}{x^2} = \frac{9}{(2 - x)^2}
$$

Cross-multiply:

$$
4(2 - x)^2 = 9x^2
$$

Take square root:

$$
2(2 - x) = 3x
$$

Solve:

$$
4 - 2x = 3x
$$

$$
4 = 5x
$$

$$
x = \frac{4}{5} = 0.8\,\text{m}
$$

## Final Answer

The equilibrium position is:

$$
\boxed{0.8\,\text{m from } q_1}
$$

(or equivalently $1.2\,\text{m}$ from $q_2$)
