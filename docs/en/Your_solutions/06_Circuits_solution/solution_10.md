# 10. Average Current

A lightning bolt transfers a charge of $30\,\text{C}$ to the ground in a time of $2\,\text{ms}$.

What is the average current of the lightning bolt?

---

# Solution

Average current is given by:

$$
I = \frac{Q}{t}
$$

where:

- $Q = 30\,\text{C}$
- $t = 2\,\text{ms}$

Convert milliseconds to seconds:

$$
2\,\text{ms} = 2 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{s}
$$

Now substitute into the formula:

$$
I = \frac{30}{2 \times 10^{-3}}
$$

$$
I = \frac{30}{0.002}
$$

$$
I = 15000\,\text{A}
$$

---

# Final Answer

$$
\boxed{
I = 1.5 \times 10^4\,\text{A}
}
$$
