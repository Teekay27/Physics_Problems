# 9. Current

Charge flowing through the wire is given by the function of time

$$
Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5
$$

What is the current at $t = 3\,\text{s}$?

---

# Solution

Current is the rate of change of charge with respect to time:

$$
I(t) = \frac{dQ}{dt}
$$

Given:

$$
Q(t) = 5t^2 + 5
$$

Differentiate with respect to $t$:

$$
I(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(5t^2 + 5)
$$

$$
I(t) = 10t
$$

Now substitute $t = 3\,\text{s}$:

$$
I(3) = 10(3)
$$

$$
I(3) = 30\,\text{A}
$$

---

# Final Answer

$$
\boxed{
I = 30\,\text{A}
}
$$
