# 11. Power and Energy in a Resistor

What is the power dissipated by a $100\,\Omega$ resistor when a voltage of $50\,\text{V}$ is applied across it? How much energy is consumed in 5 minutes?

---

# Solution

## Step 1: Calculate the Power

Power dissipated in a resistor is given by:

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

where:

- $V = 50\,\text{V}$
- $R = 100\,\Omega$

Substitute the values:

$$
P = \frac{50^2}{100}
$$

$$
P = \frac{2500}{100}
$$

$$
P = 25\,\text{W}
$$

---

# Step 2: Calculate the Energy Consumed

Energy is given by:

$$
E = Pt
$$

where:

- $P = 25\,\text{W}$
- $t = 5\,\text{minutes}$

Convert minutes to seconds:

$$
5\,\text{min} = 5 \times 60 = 300\,\text{s}
$$

Now calculate:

$$
E = 25 \times 300
$$

$$
E = 7500\,\text{J}
$$

---

# Final Answers

## Power Dissipated

$$
\boxed{
P = 25\,\text{W}
}
$$

---

## Energy Consumed in 5 Minutes

$$
\boxed{
E = 7500\,\text{J}
}
$$
