# 12. Transformer Currents

A transformer has a primary coil with 1000 turns and a secondary coil with 200 turns.

If the primary voltage is $120\,\text{V}$ (AC), what is the secondary voltage?

If the current in the secondary is $3\,\text{A}$, what is the current in the primary?

---

# Solution

## Step 1: Calculate the Secondary Voltage

For an ideal transformer:

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

where:

- $V_p = 120\,\text{V}$
- $N_p = 1000$
- $N_s = 200$

Substitute the values:

$$
\frac{V_s}{120} = \frac{200}{1000}
$$

$$
\frac{V_s}{120} = 0.2
$$

Multiply both sides by $120$:

$$
V_s = 120 \times 0.2
$$

$$
V_s = 24\,\text{V}
$$

---

# Step 2: Calculate the Primary Current

For an ideal transformer:

$$
V_p I_p = V_s I_s
$$

Rearrange to find the primary current:

$$
I_p = \frac{V_s I_s}{V_p}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
I_p = \frac{24 \times 3}{120}
$$

$$
I_p = \frac{72}{120}
$$

$$
I_p = 0.6\,\text{A}
$$

---

# Final Answers

## Secondary Voltage

$$
\boxed{
V_s = 24\,\text{V}
}
$$

---

## Primary Current

$$
\boxed{
I_p = 0.6\,\text{A}
}
$$
