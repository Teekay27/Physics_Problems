# 7. Capacitors in Parallel

Two capacitors, $C_1 = 4\,\mu\text{F}$ and $C_2 = 6\,\mu\text{F}$, are connected in parallel to a $10\,\text{V}$ battery.

Find:

1. The total charge stored on the capacitors.  
2. The total energy stored.

---

# Solution

## Step 1: Equivalent Capacitance

For capacitors in parallel:

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = C_1 + C_2
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 4\,\mu\text{F} + 6\,\mu\text{F}
$$

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 10\,\mu\text{F}
$$

Convert to farads:

$$
C_{\text{eq}} = 10 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{F}
$$

---

# Step 2: Total Charge Stored

The charge stored is:

$$
Q = CV
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
Q = (10 \times 10^{-6})(10)
$$

$$
Q = 100 \times 10^{-6}\,\text{C}
$$

$$
Q = 1.0 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{C}
$$

or

$$
Q = 100\,\mu\text{C}
$$

---

# Step 3: Total Energy Stored

Energy stored in a capacitor is:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}(10 \times 10^{-6})(10^2)
$$

$$
U = \frac{1}{2}(10 \times 10^{-6})(100)
$$

$$
U = 5 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J}
$$

or

$$
U = 0.0005\,\text{J}
$$

---

# Final Answers

## Total Charge Stored

$$
\boxed{
Q = 100\,\mu\text{C}
}
$$

---

## Total Energy Stored

$$
\boxed{
U = 5 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{J}
}
$$
