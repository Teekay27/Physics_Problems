# 13. Transformer Ratio

A transformer is used to step down the voltage from $120\,\text{V}$ AC to $9.0\,\text{V}$ AC.

If the primary coil has 400 turns, how many turns must the secondary coil have?

---

# Solution

For an ideal transformer:

$$
\frac{V_s}{V_p} = \frac{N_s}{N_p}
$$

where:

- $V_p = 120\,\text{V}$
- $V_s = 9.0\,\text{V}$
- $N_p = 400$
- $N_s = ?$

Substitute the values:

$$
\frac{9.0}{120} = \frac{N_s}{400}
$$

Simplify:

$$
0.075 = \frac{N_s}{400}
$$

Multiply both sides by $400$:

$$
N_s = 0.075 \times 400
$$

$$
N_s = 30
$$

---

# Final Answer

$$
\boxed{
N_s = 30\ \text{turns}
}
$$
