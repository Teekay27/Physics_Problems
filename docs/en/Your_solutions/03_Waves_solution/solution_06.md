## 6. Wave Equation

### Problem

A wave is described by:

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

where $x$ and $y$ are in meters and $t$ is in seconds.

Determine:

a) Amplitude $A$  
b) Wavelength $\lambda$  
c) Frequency $f$  
d) Wave speed $v$  

---

### Solution

Compare with the standard wave form:

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

---

### Step 1: Identify parameters

From the given equation:

$$
A = 0.05, \quad k = 2\pi, \quad \omega = 50\pi
$$

---

### a) Amplitude

$$
A = 0.05 \text{ m}
$$

---

### b) Wavelength

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{k}
$$

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi} = 1 \text{ m}
$$

---

### c) Frequency

$$
f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}
$$

$$
f = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

---

### d) Wave Speed

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

$$
v = 25 \times 1 = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### Final Answers

- a) $A = 0.05 \text{ m}$  
- b) $\lambda = 1 \text{ m}$  
- c) $f = 25 \text{ Hz}$  
- d) $v = 25 \text{ m/s}$
