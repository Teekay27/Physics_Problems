## 6. Wave Equation

### Problem

A wave is described by:

$$
y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)
$$

where $x$ and $y$ are in meters and $t$ is in seconds.

Determine the wave’s:
- Amplitude $A$  
- Wavelength $\lambda$  
- Frequency $f$  
- Wave speed $v$  

---

### Solution

Compare with the standard wave form:

$$
y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)
$$

---

### Step 1: Identify parameters

From the equation:

- $A = 0.05$  
- $k = 2\pi$  
- $\omega = 50\pi$  

---

### Step 2: Find Wavelength

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{k}
$$

$$
\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi} = 1 \text{ m}
$$

---

### Step 3: Find Frequency

$$
f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}
$$

$$
f = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25 \text{ Hz}
$$

---

### Step 4: Find Wave Speed

$$
v = f \lambda
$$

$$
v = 25 \times 1 = 25 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### Final Answers

- Amplitude:  
  $$
  A = 0.05 \text{ m}
  $$

- Wavelength:  
  $$
  \lambda = 1 \text{ m}
  $$

- Frequency:  
  $$
  f = 25 \text{ Hz}
  $$

- Wave speed:  
  $$
  v = 25 \text{ m/s}
  $$
